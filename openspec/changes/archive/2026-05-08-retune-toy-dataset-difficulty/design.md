## Context

The committed toy dataset at `examples/data/toy/` is generated by:

```
motco simulate --seed 42 --n-samples 90 --trajectory-mode orientation --effect-size 2.0 --out-dir examples/data/toy/
```

Two parameters jointly determine the *injected group signal*:

- `group_effect_size` — currently `2.0`. Linear scaling of the per-sample shift applied to the alternative group across stages.
- `prop_affected_features` — currently the internal default `0.1` (10% of features per omic layer). Determines how many features move in concert.

In `orientation` mode with `n_stages = 3`, group B is shifted by `effect_size × stage × effect_vector` on the affected features. With `effect_size = 2.0` and ~66 features carrying the same coordinated direction, the group difference saturates the trajectory analysis: the latent-space angle between groups A and B is ~93° with RRPP p_angle ≈ 0.005 — visually pinned at the maximum, with no room for the test to express uncertainty.

Separately, the original toy-difficulty framing assumed that `effect_size` and `prop_affected_features` would also control the difficulty of the supervised PLS-DA classification (`y = stage`) and therefore move the AUROC table. During implementation we found this assumption is false: stage classification on InterSIM-generated data is governed by InterSIM's internal `cluster_mean_shift` (which positions the cluster centroids that the toy interprets as stages), not by the group-effect knobs. With three well-separated cluster centroids and ~660 omics features, `y = stage` AUROC is trivially 1.0 regardless of any group-effect setting. The companion change `fix-plsda-nested-cv-aggregation` already made AUROC reporting honest (mean-of-K rather than max-of-K); the present change does not target it further.

The retune therefore aims to make **trajectory analysis** more realistic, not the CV table. The fix-aggregation change must merge first because honest reporting is a prerequisite for ever interpreting CV outputs from this change confidently, even though we now know the AUROC numbers themselves don't move.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- Lower the toy dataset's injected group signal so that RRPP p-values for the orientation mode are detectable but not trivially pinned (target: `0 < p_angle ≤ 0.1` on seed 42 with 199 permutations).
- Preserve the dataset's pedagogical purpose: the orientation-mode trajectory difference SHALL still be detectable by `motco de` (latent-space angle clearly above 0, but well below the prior ~90° saturation).
- Expose `prop_affected_features` as a CLI flag on `motco simulate` so users can sweep it.
- Keep the dataset shape (90 samples, three omics, three stages, two groups) and file format unchanged.

**Non-Goals:**
- Targeting the `y = stage` CV AUROC value. The notebook's PLS-DA section uses stage as the supervision signal; on InterSIM-generated data the AUROC stays near 1.0 by design and is not a tunable in scope here. (Follow-up proposal: expose `cluster_mean_shift`.)
- Changing the trajectory mode of the toy (still `orientation` for the reasons in original D2).
- Adding new omics layers or new stages.
- Adding a "difficulty" preset (`easy`/`medium`/`hard`) — kept as a future extension.
- Adding post-hoc Gaussian noise on top of InterSIM's internal noise — out of scope.
- Tuning `motco simulate` for users other than the tutorial — researchers needing fine-grained simulation control already have the Python API.

## Decisions

### D1 — Sequence after `fix-plsda-nested-cv-aggregation`

The fix-aggregation change is sequenced first because honest CV reporting is a precondition for *any* interpretation of `plsda_doubleCV` output. The two changes are otherwise independent — the present change does not modify or depend on the AUROC table.

### D2 — Tuned defaults: `effect_size = 1.0`, `prop_affected_features = 0.1`

Empirical sweep on seed 42 (n_samples=90, orientation mode, RRPP perms=199), reporting RRPP p-values for the angle/delta/shape statistics computed in the supervised PLS-DA latent space:

| effect_size | prop  | angle (°) | p_angle | delta | p_delta | shape | p_shape |
|------------:|------:|----------:|--------:|------:|--------:|------:|--------:|
| 2.0         | 0.10  |   93.4    | 0.005   | 4.62  | 0.005   | 0.081 | 0.010   |
| 2.0         | 0.05  |   10.7    | 0.695   | 3.34  | 0.005   | 0.038 | 0.290   |
| 1.5         | 0.05  |   13.2    | 0.460   | 3.21  | 0.005   | 0.034 | 0.285   |
| **1.0**     | **0.10** | **71.8** | **0.035** | **4.05** | **0.005** | **0.063** | **0.025** |
| 1.0         | 0.05  |   15.0    | 0.510   | 3.02  | 0.015   | 0.029 | 0.490   |
| 0.5         | 0.10  |   48.5    | 0.070   | 3.66  | 0.005   | 0.051 | 0.060   |
| 0.5         | 0.05  |   15.7    | 0.490   | 2.75  | 0.020   | 0.022 | 0.670   |

Key findings:

- **Lowering `prop_affected_features` below 0.10 destroys the orientation signal in the supervised PLS-DA latent space.** Across all three tested `effect_size` values, going from `prop = 0.10` to `prop = 0.05` collapses the recovered angle from ~50–90° to ~10–15° and drives `p_angle` to non-significance. This is because PLS-DA fits its latent axes against the dominant covarying signal in the input; with only ~33 affected features distributed across three omics layers, that signal is no longer the leading axis the projection captures.
- **`effect_size` alone is the right knob** to retune. At `prop = 0.10`, the angle/`p_angle` pair scales monotonically: 0.5 → (48°, 0.07), 1.0 → (72°, 0.035), 2.0 → (93°, 0.005).
- `effect_size = 1.0` was chosen as the new default. Angle ~72° (clearly orientation, but not pinned at 90°), `p_angle ≈ 0.035` (significant but not trivial), `p_shape ≈ 0.025` (also informative). This is the smallest change from the previous default that meets the realism goal.
- `--prop-affected-features` is still exposed as a flag (default `0.1`) so power users can experiment with sparser signals from the Python API or CLI without modifying their environment, but the recommended default does not change from the previous internal default.

### D3 — Add `--prop-affected-features` to `motco simulate`

The original `simulate-command` spec lists four flags: `--seed`, `--out-dir`, `--n-samples`, `--trajectory-mode`, `--effect-size`. The present change adds `--prop-affected-features` (float, `[0, 1]`, default `0.1`). Out-of-range values are caught by an early validator in `cmd_simulate` so the command does not invoke R only to fail downstream. Per-layer overrides (the Python API supports a `Mapping[OmicsLayer, float]`) are not exposed via the CLI; users needing per-layer control should use the Python API.

### D4 — Preserve `truth.json` schema

`truth.json` already records `group_effect_size`, `affected_features` (per layer), and `effect_coefficients`. With the new flag, the same structure holds; only its content shifts (`group_effect_size` becomes `1.0`, `affected_features` lengths reflect the chosen proportion). No schema change to `truth.json` is required.

### D5 — Acceptance check for the regenerated toy

Before committing the regenerated `examples/data/toy/`, the implementer SHALL run the full PLS path on the new data and confirm:

1. `motco de` (RRPP) on the resulting Y produces a non-trivial **angle** statistic between groups A and B with `0 < p_angle ≤ 0.1` (signal detectable, not pinned at zero).
2. The latent-space angle is clearly non-zero (≥ 30°) but not pinned near 90°.
3. The notebook runs end-to-end with no errors.

If these checks fail on seed 42, adjust `--effect-size` within `[0.75, 1.5]` and re-test.

The check explicitly does **not** target a specific `y = stage` AUROC band. The fix-aggregation change ensures that whatever the AUROC value is, it is honestly reported.

### D6 — `y = stage` AUROC is not a difficulty target

The notebook's supervised PLS-DA section uses `y = stage` to find a stage-aligned latent space, not to demonstrate hard classification. On InterSIM cluster-driven data, three well-separated cluster centroids → three easily-separable stages, and the resulting `y = stage` AUROC is near 1.0 by design. No knob exposed by `motco simulate` controls cluster-mean separation; that lives in InterSIM's internal `cluster_mean_shift` parameter (currently hardcoded). A separate follow-up proposal (`expose-intersim-cluster-mean-shift`) is suggested to expose this; it is out of scope here.

The notebook narrative will be updated to make this explicit so readers do not interpret a saturated AUROC as a bug.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **[Risk] Tuning-by-target makes the chosen defaults seed-dependent.** A value that hits the angle/p band on seed 42 may not hit it on a different seed. *Mitigation:* the toy is fixed at seed 42 and used by tutorial users only.
- **[Risk] Lowering signal too far hides the orientation effect.** D5's lower bound (`p_angle ≤ 0.1`) and the empirical observation that `prop_affected ≤ 0.05` collapses orientation guard against this.
- **[Risk] Changes to `examples/data/toy/` break any external references that pinned to old file content.** *Mitigation:* `truth.json` is the canonical record of what was generated; users can regenerate or pin their own dataset by calling `motco simulate` with the explicit flags they want.
- **[Trade-off] The CV table will continue to show AUROC ≈ 1.0 for every repeat.** This is now correct (honest mean-of-K reporting on an intentionally easy problem) but may surprise users who expect a "harder data" frame to move the AUROC table. The notebook narrative addresses this directly.
- **[Trade-off] The CLI surface grows by one flag.** Acceptable: `--prop-affected-features` is a real, useful knob even outside the toy retune context.
- **[Trade-off] The previous summary cell in the notebook (claiming "delta p-values are expected to be non-significant in orientation mode") is incorrect for the supervised PLS-DA latent space.** It was incorrect at the original `effect_size = 2.0` defaults too; we just didn't notice because everything was significant by saturation. The narrative refresh corrects this in passing.
